Example.

In a world where the collection of online data is increasing exponentially, the need to make sense of this growing pool of data is becoming more and more essential. In addition, the constant evolution of this world through time makes it that static representations of data at one single point in time have large limitations for our understanding of the dynamics of the data (1). Dynamic graph visualizations attempt to map this evolution of relationships between entities in the data in a clearly understandable, scalable and effective manner. In contrast to the static graph, the dynamic graph has the ability to change the structure of the vertices and edges over time (1).
